1. Executive Summary: Opportunity Narrative

Market gap: Hobbyists can design boards in KiCad/EasyEDA, but the path from Gerber files to reliable same-day physical PCB output is still fragmented by CAM setup, tooling, failed boards, and outsourced fab delays.

Beachhead: High-intent electronics makers already trying desktop PCB production with FlatCAM, pcb2gcode, generic CNC mills, Othermill/Bantam-style machines, or Voltera V-One-like workflows.

Price anchor: Prosumer fabrication buyers have paid $800-$2,300 weighted campaign prices for successful desktop tools; Raven should avoid being framed as a pure $2,000 hobby toy.

Investment thesis: Kickstarter confirms a broad paid market for desktop fabrication machines, and software telemetry confirms a specific PCB fabrication pain point. Raven is justified if it converts CAM complexity into a reliable appliance-like workflow.

Market Sentiment & Community Intelligence

Core hubs: r/PrintedCircuitBoard, r/AskElectronics, KiCad forum, EEVblog, maker YouTube, GitHub/SourceForge CAM projects, Arduino/Raspberry Pi communities.

Recurring friction: cheap fabs are slow for iteration; DIY milling is fragile; CAM/autoleveling is confusing; chemical/additive processes add storage and reliability issues.

Aha signal: Users still download FlatCAM/pcb2gcode and repurpose generic CNC machines, proving the workaround exists and the workflow is not solved.

Strategic Positioning & Moat

USP: Raven should be the simplest same-day PCB fabrication appliance for hobbyists who already design boards but do not want to become CNC/CAM experts.

Moat: integrated Gerber-to-machine software, protected autoleveling, bit/tooling recipes, board-material ecosystem, education content, and Voltera credibility in desktop electronics fabrication.

Pricing hypothesis: MSRP $1,499-$1,999; launch paid price $999-$1,499; higher bundle for fixtures, bits, blanks, and education/pro-maker accessories.

Gate 1 Decision

PASS to Phase 3 - Customer Discovery. The evidence is strong enough to test willingness-to-pay, workflows, and result-quality requirements with customers.
